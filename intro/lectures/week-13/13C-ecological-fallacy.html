What's going on?

Ecological Fallacy

The false assumption that relationships that exist at an aggregated level (e.g. between states, counties, nations) also hold at the individual level.

Immigration and Income

Freedman (1999)

Suggests that foreign-born populations tend to have higher incomes (corr = 0.52).

Immigration and Income

  • correlation = -0.05

What's going on?

  • Unit of observation: state
  • Explanation: states with many foreign-born people have natives with higher incomes.

Ecological Fallacy

The false assumption that relationships that exist at an aggregated level (e.g. between states, counties, nations) also hold at the individual level.

Interpretations can invert due to:

  • confounding / omitted variable (Model 1)
  • aggregation (Model 2)

Strategies

  1. When dealing with an ecological study, restrict model interpretations to the same observational level on which the data was collected.

  2. If you want to make individual-level inferences, be sure your data is collected on that same level. Aggregate-level effects can be introduced via a multi-level model.
